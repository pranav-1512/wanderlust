About

A medieval fort overlooking the rest of city, Golconda is one of the historical monuments to visit in Hyderabad. The fort is constructed of mud on a hill nearly 400ft high. The fort derives its name from a Telugu word, which means Shepherd’s Hill. The Golconda Fort houses four different Forts. The striking feature of the fort includes the relics of armories, multiple fortification layers, mosques, reservoirs, granaries, audience chambers, and royal dwellings. One of the most amazing features of the fort is its acoustics, which is a unique signaling device incorporated in its construction. The light and sound show at the fort adds grandeur to it. The structure attracts visitors for its palaces, the famous Fateh Rahben - one of the cannons used by Aurangzeb and ingenious water supply system.

Best Time To Visit

Although Golconda Fort can be visited at any time of the year, yet it is advisable to tourists to plan their tour to the fort during the winter months of October to February. As the weather of the city is very pleasant during this period, tourists are likely to have a good time at the fort. Visitors are suggested to head to the fort during the evening hours so that they can enjoy the popular light and sound show. Tourists should avoid visiting Golconda Fort during the summer afternoons as it is extremely hot and humid during this time.

Charminar Ticket Booking

The price of the entry ticket to Golconda fort is INR 15 for Indian nationals and INR 200 for foreign nationals.
Timings: Golconda Fort is open for visitors from 8 AM to 5.30 PM, throughout the week

Visitor's Facility

A plethora of decent and budget-friendly food joints are available near the fort where visitors to Golconda Fort can have a whole-hearted meal. In addition, there are also a host of good hotels at proximate distance to the fort where tourists can unwind and relax for the day.

How to Reach Golconda

Nearest bus stand to Golconda:
Golconda Fort is located at an approximate distance of around 12.3 kilometers from Mahatma Gandhi Bus Station, Hyderabad.
